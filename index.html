What is the Present Continuous?

The Present Continuous is for actions *in progress* right now. Think of it as a live video stream of your life! It's made with two simple parts: the verb **'to be'** ($am, is, are$) + the main verb with **-ing**.

When Do We Use It? üí°

This section explains the different situations where you can use the Present Continuous. Click on each use to see a personalized example that connects to your life and interests!

Action Happening Now

For something happening at this exact moment.
Example: I am playing volleyball now. üèê

Temporary Action

Happening around now, but won't last forever.
Example: She is listening to Adele at the moment. üé∂

Future Plan

Something already decided for the near future.
Example: We are watching a drama today. üé•

Dreams & Wishes

Something you are focusing on right now.
Example: I am dreaming about Italy right now. üáÆüáπ

Speaking Task: What are you doing? üé§

This is a fun game to play with a partner or family member to practice your speaking and listening skills. Follow the steps below!

  1. Mime: Secretly choose an action (like eating spaghetti or singing) and act it out without making a sound.
  2. Guess: Your partner asks "Are you...?" questions to guess your action. (e.g., "Are you reading a book?").
  3. Answer: Reply with "Yes, I am!" or "No, I'm not." until they guess correctly. Then switch roles!
